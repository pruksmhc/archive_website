@Yada Pruksachatkun

According to the World Health Organization, one in four of people worldwide will go through a mental or neurological disorder at some point in their lives.

As an engineer, I'm on a mission to help people get help faster

For many, they cannot get help and support they need.

Stigmaziation and shortage of psychiatrists and therapists have led to this world-wide shortage of mental healthcare support. How can we make the current traditional hospital system more efficient and reduce the time it takes for patients to get help? How can we make alternative methods like online mental health forums more efficient - via community moderation? With my research, I'm currently finding semi-automatic ways to crowdsource and redirect expert community moderators to forums in the web that need the most help - whether that be forums in mental health forums that are not progressing and helping the poster, or instances of cyberbullying in the web. I worked on a project to enable psychiatric clinics to handle more patients by using technology to optimize time sinks such as that during intake, which is resulting in an IRB-approved research study at Northwell Health system. By doing so, we ensure people who reach out for help in the hospital system are able to get access to healthcare faster (less wait times, less hospitals with no open patient slots).

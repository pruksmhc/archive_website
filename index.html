ActiveRank is an open-source chrome extension that makes data on women in the workplace more interactive. With a click of a button, you can find the pay gap, maternity leave benefits, and percentage of women of the company you are currently browsing online. By making data more transparent, we aim to hold companies accountable to equality among employees and equip female job-seekers with a tool to find out how they may be treated at a company. As featured in WECode (biggest collegiate conference for women in Computer Science) and ReigningIt.
How prone are search engines to attackers during the 2016 presidential elections? Over the span of 6 months, we collected data on search engine results of 340 political candidates in Google, Bing, and Yahoo, and measured the vulnerability of each search engine. Research published as part of the ICIW (International Conference on Internet and Web Applications and Services).
What if we could control our audio landscape? As someone who frequently has conversations in loud places, I have many times wondered if it was possible to choose to focus on a person, and zone out the rest. That is exactly what I did as a final project for the MIT graduate class Nature of Mathematical Modeling. I did a deep dive into three approaches - independent component analysis (ICA), deep learning, and compressive sensing, on a single audio file. With the MIT Sandbox Fellowship, I am working on converting the algorithm into an app that gives you the ability to control what you do or don't hear with any pair of earhones and phones.
Can you detect who is misusing a service? Using SVMs, I created an algorithm for a consumer sports startup that detected and banned users with 97% accuracy. Here is a Github respo outlining the process and final result.
Can you predict Airbnb's user adoption? Using Bayesian survival analysis, a friend and I modeled the number of houses in Airbnb over time in the state of New York.
